PEB Engineering Mar 15, 2026

Why Pre-Engineered Buildings Are Replacing Traditional Construction in India

For decades, traditional RCC (Reinforced Cement Concrete) was the standard for industrial infrastructure in India. However, the paradigm is shifting rapidly toward Pre-Engineered Buildings (PEB). At PRS GROUP, we've seen a 300% increase in PEB demand over the last 3 years.

1. Compressed Timelines

Traditional construction can take up to 18-24 months for large-scale factories. PEB systems are fabricated in a controlled environment while foundations are being laid on-site. This parallel workflow reduces total project time by nearly 50%.

2. Structural Precision

Using CAD/CAM software, every bolt hole and weld is precisely engineered to within 1mm. This eliminates the "on-site error margin" that often plagues traditional construction, leading to lower maintenance costs over decades.

3. Scalability

PEB systems are essentially giant "Meccano" sets. As your industrial capacity grows, adding additional bays or mezzanine levels is a matter of simple bolted expansion, rather than destructive demolition.

Anti-Corrosive Coating Systems: Epoxy vs. Polyurethane for Blast Furnace Environments

In high-stakes environments like NINL Kalinganagar or TATA STEEL BSL, corrosion is the primary enemy of structural longevity. Choosing the right coating system is not just about aesthetics — it's about multi-year asset protection.

Epoxy: The Foundation of Protection

Epoxies are prized for their exceptional adhesion and chemical resistance. In our execution for NINL, we utilize High-Build Epoxy primers that create a dense, moisture-impermeable barrier. However, epoxies can "chalk" under direct UV exposure.

Polyurethane (PU): The UV Defense

PU coatings act as the ultimate topcoat. They are highly resistant to UV radiation and provide a gloss finish that resists weathering. At PRS GROUP, we recommend a three-tier system: Zinc-Rich Primer + Epoxy Intermediate + PU Finish Coat for maximum 10-year durability.
